ABOUT

Vulfpeck, an American funk band formed in 2011, has captivated music enthusiasts with their distinctive blend of funk, soul, and rhythm. Hailing from Ann Arbor, Michigan, the band gained initial recognition through their clever marketing tactics and a strong presence on online platforms. Vulfpeck's music embodies a nostalgic yet fresh sound, drawing inspiration from classic funk and R&B while infusing it with their own quirky and playful style.


The band's lineup consists of multi-talented musicians, including Jack Stratton, Theo Katzman, Joe Dart, Woody Goss, and other occasional collaborators. What sets Vulfpeck apart is not only their remarkable musical talent but also their innovative approach to releasing and promoting their music. They garnered attention by creating a silent album titled "Sleepify," encouraging fans to stream the album overnight on repeat to generate royalties. This quirky strategy not only earned them a significant amount of money but also solidified their reputation as a band that thinks outside the box.


Vulfpeck's discography is a testament to their mastery of groovy basslines, infectious melodies, and tight-knit arrangements. Their tracks, such as "Dean Town" and "1612," showcase the band's ability to create irresistibly catchy tunes that transcend traditional genre boundaries. Their live performances are equally captivating, characterized by high-energy jams and a strong rapport with the audience. With a growing fanbase and an ever-evolving musical journey, Vulfpeck continues to be a refreshing and influential force in the modern music landscape.
